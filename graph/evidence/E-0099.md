# E-0099 — Defense AI investment

The **Department of Defense's Chief Digital and Artificial Intelligence Office** (CDAO, established 2022) is DOD's principal hub for AI capability adoption and integration across the department, absorbing and operationalizing DARPA-developed AI capabilities for departmental deployment. **DARPA's AI Next campaign**, launched in 2018, has invested **more than $2 billion** in advancing AI for national security purposes; the successor **AI Forward** initiative focuses on trustworthiness for national-security AI systems.

For C-0038: defense AI investment runs in parallel with the civilian programs (NAIRR, NSF AI Institutes, DOE Genesis Mission). The combined federal AI funding envelope — civilian + defense — is the largest sustained federal commitment to a single research-and-development priority since the post-Sputnik space program. Institutions with credible AI capabilities are competing for both pools; institutions without them are competing for neither.

E-0064 captures the aggregate scale (~$3.3B/yr nondefense + $2B+ DARPA + $320M+ DOE Genesis Mission); this evidence node disaggregates the defense component.
